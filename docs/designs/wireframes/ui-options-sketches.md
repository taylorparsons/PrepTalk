# PrepTalk UI Redesign: Wireframe Sketches

Three design direction options for transforming PrepTalk from a cluttered dashboard to a focused, calming interview coach experience.

---

## Option A: "Zen Flow"

### Design Rationale
Inspired by meditation apps like Headspace and Calm. Embraces extreme minimalism with a single focal point per screen. Uses whitespace as a design element to reduce cognitive load. The interface breathes, giving users mental space to focus on their practice.

### Color Mood
- **Warm Earth Tones**: Soft cream (#fff9f1), warm sage green (#1f6f5f), muted gold accents (#e0a03b)
- Background gradients: Subtle warm-to-cool transitions
- Text: Deep charcoal for readability without harshness

### Emotional Need Addressed
**Anxiety Reduction** - Users who freeze during interviews need calm, not more stimulation. The minimal interface says "breathe, you've got this."

| Easy to understand flow | Requires more screens/clicks |
| Highly accessible | May not show enough context |
| Mobile-first friendly | Power users may want more |

## Option B: "Warm Guide"

### Design Rationale
Inspired by Duolingo and friendly onboarding experiences. Uses illustrated characters or icons to create warmth and approachability. Card-based layout provides structure while maintaining playfulness. Micro-copy is encouraging and conversational.

### Color Mood
- **Warm Sunset Palette**: Soft coral, warm peach, friendly teal accents
- Illustrated elements with rounded, friendly shapes
- Backgrounds: Subtle patterns or textures for warmth

### Emotional Need Addressed
**Encouragement & Companionship** - Users feel alone in interview prep. A friendly guide character makes them feel supported, like having a coach in their corner.

## Option C: "Confident Pro"

### Design Rationale
Inspired by professional tools like Linear, Notion, and premium productivity apps. Clean typography-first design with strong visual hierarchy. Progress is celebrated through subtle, confident indicators rather than gamification. The interface conveys competence and credibility.

### Color Mood
- **Confident Navy & Warm Neutrals**: Deep navy (#1a2744), warm cream, subtle gold accents
- Typography-driven design with excellent contrast
- Minimal decoration, maximum clarity

### Emotional Need Addressed
**Competence & Credibility** - Users need to feel they're using a serious tool that will actually help them succeed. Professional design builds trust that the coaching is legitimate.

## Comparison Matrix

| Aspect | A: Zen Flow | B: Warm Guide | C: Confident Pro |
|--------|-------------|---------------|------------------|
| **Primary Emotion** | Calm | Encouraged | Confident |
| **Visual Style** | Minimal | Illustrated | Typography-driven |
| **Information Density** | Low | Medium | High |
| **Whitespace** | Maximum | Balanced | Efficient |
| **Progress Indicators** | Subtle | Gamified | Data-driven |
| **Tone of Voice** | Serene | Friendly | Professional |
| **Best For** | Anxious users | First-time users | Experienced professionals |
| **Risk** | Too sparse | Too casual | Too cold |
| **Mobile Friendliness** | Excellent | Good | Fair |
| **Accessibility** | Excellent | Good | Good |

---

## Recommendation

Consider a **hybrid approach** that combines:

1. **Zen Flow's** whitespace and calm during practice (the core anxiety-inducing moment)
2. **Warm Guide's** encouraging tone and progress indicators throughout the journey
3. **Confident Pro's** clear typography hierarchy and professional feedback presentation

This would create an interface that:
- Feels professional and credible (builds trust)
- Remains calm and focused during practice (reduces anxiety)
- Celebrates progress warmly (encourages return visits)
- Respects users' intelligence while supporting their emotional needs

2. Prototype one option (or hybrid) for usability testing
3. A/B test different tones of voice with real users
4. Develop component library based on chosen direction
